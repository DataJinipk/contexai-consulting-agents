# OGRA Upgrade Agreement Drafting Guide — BRP-21 Compliance

## Background
The Brownfield Refining Policy 2021 (BRP-21) — issued by Ministry of Energy (MoE) Petroleum Division — provides a 10-year corporate tax holiday + 10% deemed customs duty on Motor Gasoline (PMG/MS-92 Euro V) and 2.5% on High-Speed Diesel (HSD/Euro V) for refineries that **invest at least USD 1 bn in upgrade projects** to meet Euro V product specifications.

OGRA (Oil and Gas Regulatory Authority) is the implementing regulator. Each refinery must sign a bilateral **Upgrade Agreement** with OGRA documenting:
- Baseline production data (pre-upgrade reference)
- Investment commitment + milestones
- Tax holiday eligibility computation
- Deemed duty allocation methodology
- Monitoring and verification mechanism
- Default and termination

## Critical Article Map

### Article 1 — Definitions
- "Upgrade Investment" — net capex above PKR 80-100M (typical threshold), excluding routine maintenance, capitalised T&I, replacement-in-kind
- "Baseline Production" — the most critical contested term. Refineries argue 50% (historical utilisation) reference; FBR/OGRA argues 75-80% (nameplate-adjusted)
- "Upgraded Capacity" — proportion of throughput attributable to upgrade vs pre-existing
- "Euro V Compliant Volume" — KBPD of PMG/HSD meeting EN 590 / EN 228 specs

### Article 2 — Tax Holiday Eligibility
- 10-year holiday from "Completion Date" (defined as Provisional Acceptance per FIDIC EPC contract)
- Pro-rata allocation: holiday applies to ratio of (Upgraded Capacity ÷ Total Production)
- **Pakistan-specific risk**: FBR has historically challenged this proportionality, demanding higher demonstrable upgrade benefit
- Recommend: Lock pro-rata formula with FBR-binding ruling pre-FID

### Article 3 — Deemed Duty Allocation
- 10% deemed duty allocation to PMG output, 2.5% to HSD
- Mechanism: OGRA pricing formula adjusts ex-refinery price upward by deemed-duty quantum; refinery retains differential as upgrade fund
- **Critical drafting point**: Deemed duty applies to **Upgrade Volume Only** vs **Total Production** — vast difference in refinery economics
- Recommend: Clarify upfront via tripartite letter (OGRA + FBR + Refinery) before signing

### Article 4 — Baseline Determination (MOST CONTESTED)
- Baseline production = average of last 3 calendar years before upgrade announcement
- "Stranded baseline" mechanism: If pre-upgrade utilisation was depressed by external factors (FX scarcity FY23, BRP-21 announcement uncertainty), refinery may invoke "stranded baseline" override
- Cnergyico-specific: FY23-25 ran at 50-55% utilisation due to FX cap on crude imports; baseline arguably distorted; SIFC settlement Aug-2025 ratified 50% baseline reference

### Article 5 — Investment Commitment & Milestones
- Total committed capex (USD)
- Phasing schedule (Phase 1/2/3)
- Bank guarantee / parent company guarantee for completion
- Step-in rights for OGRA if Contractor fails

### Article 6 — Monitoring & Verification (M&V)
- Quarterly production reports to OGRA (refinery-witnessed by independent metering firm — Bureau Veritas, SGS, Intertek)
- Annual Euro V compliance certification (PSQCA + OGRA panel auditor)
- 3rd-party capex verification (Big-4 firm cost certification)
- Random inspection rights

### Article 7 — Tax Holiday Computation (Worked Example)
For a refinery with:
- Baseline: 80 KBPD (50% of 160 KBPD nameplate)
- Post-upgrade: 130 KBPD (81% of nameplate)
- Upgrade fraction: (130-80)/130 = 38.5% → 38.5% of corporate income tax-exempt for 10 yrs

Alternative formulation (Euro V spec-compliance argument):
- 100% of post-upgrade production is Euro V-compliant
- Therefore 100% of post-upgrade production is "Upgraded Production"
- Tax holiday applies to 100% of incremental profit

**The two formulations give very different NPVs** — drafting precision matters by ~USD 200-400M NPV.

### Article 8 — Default & Force Majeure
- Default if Contractor abandons site >180 days, fails to achieve PAC within 24 months of target
- Force majeure scope: wars, regulatory change, sanctions, pandemics; payable to Contractor (relief on time only, not cost)
- Termination payoff: Net Book Value of plant at Termination + reasonable lost profit

### Article 9 — Dispute Resolution
- Step 1: OGRA Tariff Determination (10 days)
- Step 2: Petition to MoE Petroleum Division (30 days)
- Step 3: International Arbitration under UNCITRAL rules, Karachi or Dubai seat (12-18 months)
- Sovereign Risk: Government-Pakistan-as-co-party where applicable

## Pakistan Drafting Pitfalls
1. **Don't accept Baseline Determination clause without escape hatch** — insist on dispute-resolution-friendly drafting
2. **Don't accept Deemed Duty applied to Total Production formula** — argues 80-100% to upgrade fraction
3. **Don't accept FBR ruling subject to "subsequent withdrawal"** — insist on tax holiday vesting and grand-fathered rate
4. **Get the Companies (Allocation of Tax) Rules tax-allocation formula in writing pre-FID** — FBR-binding
5. **Watch S4C Super Tax interaction** — historically refineries argued exemption; FBR may litigate
6. **Carbon credit eligibility** — silent or excluded in most drafts; if refinery generates carbon credits from Euro V switch, ownership matters

## Recent Precedents
- **Cnergyico Aug-2025 SIFC settlement**: PKR 47.5 bn Petroleum Levy dispute resolved via Cabinet Committee; settlement framework references BRP-21 Article 4 baseline interpretation in refinery's favor.
- **PARCO FY24 OGRA negotiation**: Successful "stranded baseline" invocation; PARCO retained 60% utilisation reference vs FBR's demanded 75%.
- **NRL**: Drafted multi-tranche tax holiday tied to phased upgrade — different completion dates trigger different holiday start dates.
