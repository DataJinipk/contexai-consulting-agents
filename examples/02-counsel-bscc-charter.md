## Article I — Authority

The Board Sustainability Climate Committee ("BSCC" or the "Committee") is constituted by the Board of Directors (the "Board") of ABC Refinery Pk Limited (the "Company") pursuant to:

(a) Section 192 of the Companies Act 2017 (Pakistan), which empowers the Board to delegate certain functions to committees of the Board;

(b) Regulation 25 of the Listed Companies (Code of Corporate Governance) Regulations 2019, which prescribes minimum board-committee governance standards for listed companies;

(c) SECP SRO 1083(I)/2024, which mandates IFRS Sustainability Standards (including IFRS S1 and IFRS S2) for Pakistani listed companies for reporting periods beginning on or after 1 July 2026; and

(d) Such other applicable laws, regulations, and Stock Exchange listing requirements as may apply from time to time.

This Charter is supplementary to, and does not derogate from, the Board's reserved matters or any other committee's terms of reference.

---

## Article II — Purpose

The purpose of the Committee is to:

1. **Provide Board-level oversight** of all matters related to sustainability, climate-related risks and opportunities, environmental, social, and governance performance, and the Company's transition plan;

2. **Approve the Company's climate-related disclosures** prepared under IFRS S1, IFRS S2, the TCFD legacy framework, and any voluntary frameworks adopted by the Company;

3. **Review and recommend to the Board** the Company's annual climate-related risk register, including scenario assumptions and time-horizon definitions;

4. **Oversee the quantification methodology** for the Company's Scope 1, 2, and 3 greenhouse gas inventory;

5. **Review the Company's progress** against published climate-related targets and recommend remedial actions where necessary;

6. **Engage with external assurance providers** on the scope, depth, and findings of any limited or reasonable assurance engagement on the Company's climate-related disclosures.

---

## Article III — Composition

3.1 The Committee shall consist of not fewer than three (3) members.

3.2 At least two (2) members of the Committee shall be Independent Non-Executive Directors as defined under the Listed Companies (Code of Corporate Governance) Regulations 2019. The Chair of the Committee shall be an Independent Non-Executive Director.

3.3 The Chief Executive Officer shall attend Committee meetings ex officio but shall not be a member of the Committee and shall not vote.

3.4 The Chief Financial Officer, the Chief Operating Officer, the Head of Health, Safety and Environment, the Head of Investor Relations, and external assurance providers (when engaged) may be invited to attend Committee meetings in such capacity as the Committee may from time to time determine.

3.5 The Company Secretary shall act as Secretary to the Committee and shall maintain minutes of all Committee meetings.

---

## Article IV — Frequency and Quorum

4.1 The Committee shall meet not fewer than four (4) times in each fiscal year. The annual meeting cycle shall include at least one meeting before each Board sitting at which climate-related disclosures, the annual sustainability report, or material climate-related decisions are to be considered.

4.2 The quorum for Committee meetings shall be two (2) members, of whom at least one shall be an Independent Non-Executive Director.

4.3 Meetings may be conducted in person, by video-conference, by telephone, or by any other means permitted under the Companies Act 2017 and the Company's Articles of Association.

4.4 Decisions of the Committee shall be by simple majority of members present and voting. In the event of a tie, the Chair shall have a casting vote.

4.5 Circular resolutions (resolutions in writing signed by all members) shall have the same effect as a resolution passed at a duly convened meeting of the Committee.

---

## Article V — Remit (detailed)

The Committee shall, without limitation:

(a) **Review and approve** the Company's annual Climate-Related Financial Disclosure (or whatever document is published under IFRS S2);

(b) **Review and approve** any material amendment to the Company's transition plan;

(c) **Review the Company's annual Scope 1, 2, and 3 greenhouse gas inventory** and the verification opinion thereon;

(d) **Review the Company's annual climate-related risk register** and approve any changes to the methodology used to construct it;

(e) **Recommend to the Board** the engagement (or non-engagement) of an external assurance provider in respect of the Company's climate-related disclosures, the scope of such engagement, and the fee envelope;

(f) **Receive reports** from management on the implementation of the OE Roadmap, the brownfield upgrade, and the petrochemicals integration thesis, insofar as those programmes affect the Company's climate-related performance;

(g) **Receive reports** from the Head of HSE on incidents potentially material to the Company's climate-related disclosure (Tier-1 LOPC events; major methane releases; etc.);

(h) **Engage with regulators** (SECP, PSX, Sindh Environmental Protection Agency) on climate-related matters as may from time to time be required.
