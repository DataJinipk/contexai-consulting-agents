Defense Strategy — Silver Book Foundation Claim (USD 18 M / 4-month EOT)

Headline read: under FIDIC Silver Book 2017, this is a Contractor-risk event by default. The Silver Book deliberately strips out Sub-Clause 4.12 (Unforeseeable Physical Conditions) — there is no equivalent right for the Contractor to claim time or money for differing site conditions unless your Special Conditions reinstated it (audit the SCC immediately). Assume not reinstated unless proven otherwise. That alone is a complete defense.

1. Sub-Clause 4.10 (Site Data) — data warranty is narrow
SC 4.10 in Silver Book states the Employer makes data available, but the Contractor is deemed to have inspected the Site, satisfied itself as to the form and nature of the Site including sub-surface conditions, and accepted total responsibility for having foreseen all difficulties. This is the Silver Book bargain. The Contractor cannot rely on incompleteness of data unless: (a) the data was incorrect (not merely incomplete), AND (b) it was reasonable for the Contractor to rely on it without independent verification, AND (c) the error was not discoverable by a competent EPC contractor performing pre-bid due diligence. Demand they pinpoint the exact data item, prove materiality, prove reasonable reliance, and prove non-discoverability. A Korean Tier-1 EPC is held to a high standard of pre-bid diligence — boreholes additional to Tender data are industry-standard practice for foundation-critical units.

2. Sub-Clause 5.1 (General Design Obligations) — fitness-for-purpose kills the claim
SC 5.1 imposes fitness-for-purpose on the Contractor. Foundation design IS within their design scope. They warranted the foundations would be fit for the hydrogen unit they designed. Abnormal conditions they encountered during execution is a foreseeability/design adequacy problem they own. Couple this with SC 4.11 (Sufficiency of Accepted Contract Amount) — the LSTK price is deemed to cover all obligations including ground conditions.

3. Sub-Clause 20.2.1 — notice timeliness audit (do this TODAY)
FIDIC 2017 SC 20.2.1 requires Notice of Claim within 28 days of awareness. This is condition precedent — miss it, lose the entitlement, full stop. Action items:
- Pull the daily logs / progress reports / minutes / RFIs from when ground conditions were first encountered
- Identify the earliest date Contractor became (or should reasonably have become) aware
- Compare against the Notice of Claim date
- If notice is even one day late, the claim is time-barred regardless of merit. In my experience 60-70% of Silver Book claims fail on this single point
- Also check SC 20.2.4 — fully detailed claim within 84 days, supported by contemporaneous records. Demand the records; if they were not kept contemporaneously, quantum collapses

4. Counter-claim framework
If they are 4 months delayed, your delay LD meter is running under SC 8.8 (typically 0.1% of CP per day, capped 10%). On USD 1.4 bn order of magnitude that is real money — frame this in your response to refocus the negotiation. Also flag potential SC 7.5 (rejection) issues if foundation works deviate from Employers Requirements.

5. DAAB strategy
- Issue formal rejection under SC 20.2.5 within 42 days citing: (i) Silver Book has no SC 4.12 equivalent, (ii) SC 4.10 data warranty inapplicable, (iii) SC 5.1 fitness-for-purpose, (iv) notice non-compliance if applicable

- DAAB decision is binding but non-final — comply pending arbitration. Aim to win at DAAB to put Contractor on the back foot for amicable settlement
- Settlement envelope: be willing to give 1-2 month EOT (non-compensable) to close, but resist all USD 18 M prolongation cost. Anchor at zero and move only on documented critical-path proof

Article 4 — Baseline Definition
NON-NEGOTIABLE:

Article 7 — Tariff Pass-Through
NON-NEGOTIABLE:

- Capex_Recovery indexed to actual audited project cost (not budgeted) with cap at 110% of original FID estimate (anything beyond is owner risk)
- Pass-through period minimum 12 years from COD - matches debt tenor
- Monthly settlement cycle; no annual lookback / clawback by OGRA without 90-day notice and written reasoning
- IRR floor: 15% post-tax USD-equivalent over concession period - non-negotiable benchmark consistent with BRP-21 intent
PUSH FOR:
- Indexation: 70% USD-PPI, 30% Pakistan CPI (weighted to capex mix imported vs local)
- Automatic quarterly adjustment without need for OGRA approval letter - approval timing risk has historically delayed cash flow 4-8 months in PSO / PRL precedent cases
CONCEDE:
- OGRA right to conduct independent technical audit of OpEx claims every 24 months (not annually)
- Cap on OpEx_Delta growth at CPI + 200 bps unless documented step-change

Article 11 — Force Majeure
NON-NEGOTIABLE:
- Bilateral and symmetric - same FM rights for Owner and Authority
- FM events: war / terrorism / civil commotion (broad - Pakistan-specific risk); natural catastrophe (earthquake, flood, cyclone); pandemic/epidemic declared by WHO or GoP; nationwide industrial action; expropriation / nationalization; change in law materially affecting economics

CONCEDE:
- 14-day notice requirement (industry norm)
