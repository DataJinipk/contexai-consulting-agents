# Saudi Aramco Downstream Entry Playbook — Cross-Border M&A Pattern

## Strategic Logic
Aramco's downstream international strategy targets ~10 MMBPD of refining capacity globally (vs ~4-5 MMBPD historic, growing toward 8-10 MMBPD by 2030). Each entry pursues 3-4 of these value pools:
1. **Crude offtake security** — captive demand pull for Saudi grades (Arab Light/Heavy/Medium), particularly important for sour grades that command premiums in complex refineries.
2. **Refining margin capture** — equity participation in growing-demand markets (Asia, MENA, Africa).
3. **Petrochemical integration** — pull-through to SABIC offtake (post-2020 acquisition), particularly olefins and aromatics integration with refining.
4. **Retail brand presence** — Aramco's "tasawk" + private-label gasoline brands; long-term consumer-tier capture.
5. **Trading optimisation** — Aramco Trading Co (ATC) gets paper + physical optimization on partner refineries' product slates.

## Pattern Recognition — Aramco's Typical Term Sheet
**Equity**: 20-40% minority for crude-supply-led deals; 50-100% for "anchor" assets (Motiva). Above 50% — full management control + Aramco directors >50% of board.

**Crude supply commitment**: 25-100% of host refinery's slate; long tenor 15-25 yr; pricing **Arab Light Asia OSP** for Asia, **Arab Medium Med OSP** for Mediterranean; freight via Aramco Trading Co's affreightment.

**Pricing protection**: Minimum offtake floor or "take-or-pay" clause; force majeure carve-outs; political-risk allocation typically 50/50.

**Tech and IP**: Aramco brings T-Star / S-Sat catalysts (S-Oil joint patents), proprietary FCC additives; license fee waived for affiliate refineries.

**Investment trigger**: Aramco's investment typically requires (a) committed expansion/upgrade capex of USD 1-3bn at host site, (b) integration with petchem (SABIC license preferred), (c) demonstrable margin uplift from Arab crude vs incumbent slate.

## Pakistan-Specific Entry Vectors
**Vector 1: Cnergyico (BRP-21 upgrade)** — Aramco supplies Arab Medium crude at OSP-discount in exchange for crude-supply security + minority equity post-upgrade. Allows Aramco to position for SPM-2 access (deep-water VLCC capable). 20-30% equity, USD 250-400M ticket.

**Vector 2: PARCO Mid-Country expansion** — PARCO is 60% Government of Pakistan, 40% Abu Dhabi (Mubadala). Aramco entry would require Mubadala exit or dilution. More complex but strategic — PARCO controls 1,000 km Karachi-Mahmood Kot pipeline + Mahmood Kot tank farm + Multan demand pull.

**Vector 3: Greenfield petrochem JV with SIFC** — Aramco + Pakistan Government via SIFC; build new Karachi-area petrochemical (cracker + polyolefin complex) anchored on Arab Light feedstock. USD 5-10bn over 5-7 yrs.

**Vector 4: Retail JV** — Aramco "tasawk" entry to Pakistan retail; partner with existing OMC (Cnergyico, PSO, Shell Pakistan post-Wafi exit). Lower-capex, faster brand visibility; offtake-pull mechanism for Aramco product placements.

## Pakistan's Negotiating Leverage
1. **Market access** — 28-32 MTPA combined refined product demand growing 4-6%/yr; gateway to Central Asia (CPEC) and Afghanistan.
2. **Sovereign relationship** — KSA-Pakistan deep bilateral ties; Aramco entry carries diplomatic weight (Hajj/Umrah commitments, deferred-oil-payment facilities, USD 5bn KSA support of FX reserves Sep-2023).
3. **Crude routing** — Pakistan as a tertiary hub for KSA crude flows to Western China + Central Asia via overland CPEC.
4. **SIFC sponsorship** — SIFC's Defence-Cabinet-led approval can move at 6-9 months (vs 18-36 months for standalone FDI) — major time-to-deal advantage for Aramco.

## Pakistan's Asks Typically Bundled
- **Crude supply differential** (vs spot or OSP) — typically USD 0.5-2.0/bbl discount on long-tenor commitment
- **Deferred payment facility** — 90-180 day terms (e.g., KSA Saudi Fund for Development frame)
- **Tech transfer** — Aramco/SABIC catalyst manufacturing JV in Pakistan
- **Local content** — 40-50% Pakistani content in any greenfield EPC
- **Knowledge transfer** — secondments, 200-500 Pakistani engineers trained at Dhahran/KAUST
