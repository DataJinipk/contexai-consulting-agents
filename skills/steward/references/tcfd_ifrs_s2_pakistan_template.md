# TCFD / IFRS S2 Climate Disclosure — Pakistan Implementation Template

## Framework Migration: TCFD → IFRS S2
- **TCFD** (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures) — 2017-2024, disbanded after IFRS Foundation absorbed mandate
- **IFRS S2** Climate-related Disclosures — issued June 2023 by ISSB; mandatory for reporting periods on or after 1 Jan 2024
- **Pakistan adoption**: SECP-mandated for listed companies via SRO 1083(I)/2024; full IFRS S2 compliance required for FY26 annual reports onwards. KSE-30 first wave (FY25 voluntary, FY26 mandatory).

## Four-Pillar Disclosure Structure

### Pillar 1: Governance
**What to disclose**:
- Board-level oversight: Climate committee or designated risk committee charter
- Management responsibility: CSO / Chief Sustainability Officer reporting line (typically CFO or CEO direct)
- Climate KPIs in executive compensation: % weight, metrics used, vesting period
- Frequency of board climate-related agenda items (typically quarterly)

**Pakistan O&G Reality Check**:
- Mari Petroleum: Board Sustainability Committee (chair = Independent Director); CSO reports to CEO
- OGDCL: HSE&E Committee at board level; climate sub-committee under formation
- PSO: No standalone climate committee; climate folded into Risk Management Committee
- Recommendation: Establish Board Climate Committee within Risk Committee; include 1 independent climate-skilled director; quarterly cadence

### Pillar 2: Strategy
**What to disclose**:
- Climate-related risks and opportunities identified (short/medium/long-term horizon — define each)
- Impact on business model, value chain, strategy, financial planning
- **Scenario analysis** — typically 2°C and 1.5°C aligned (NGFS scenarios; IEA Net Zero Emissions; IPCC RCP 8.5)
- Climate transition plan — Pakistan O&G typically aligned with national NDC (Nationally Determined Contribution) commitments

**Pakistan NDC Context**:
- Pakistan NDC (revised 2021): 50% reduction in projected emissions by 2030 (conditional + unconditional); 60% renewable in installed power capacity by 2030; bans coal power post-2030
- Vulnerability: Pakistan ranked among top-10 most climate-vulnerable nations (Climate Risk Index, 2024)
- Physical risk: 2022 floods caused USD 30 bn damage = 9% of GDP; recurring monsoon disruption affects refinery cooling water, jetty operations, gas transmission

**Recommended Scenarios for Pakistan O&G Disclosure**:
| Horizon | Scenario | Key Variables |
|---|---|---|
| Short-term (≤3 yr) | NGFS Current Policies | Brent USD 60-90, modest carbon price USD 5-15/tCO₂, no Pakistan ETS yet |
| Medium-term (3-10 yr) | NGFS Delayed Transition | Brent USD 50-80, EU CBAM impact on Pakistan exports, carbon price USD 30-60/tCO₂ in trading partner markets |
| Long-term (10-30 yr) | NGFS Net Zero 2050 | Oil demand peak ~2030, refining margins compress, Pakistan refining sector consolidation likely |

### Pillar 3: Risk Management
**What to disclose**:
- Process to identify, assess, manage climate-related risks
- Integration into overall enterprise risk management (ERM) framework
- Time horizons: Short (<3 yr), Medium (3-10 yr), Long (>10 yr)

**Risk Categories (IFRS S2 SB6)**:

*Physical Risks*
- Acute: Floods (2022 precedent), heat waves disrupting operations, cyclones in Karachi/Hub corridor
- Chronic: Water scarcity (Indus basin stress), heat increasing cooling water requirements, sea-level rise affecting coastal refineries

*Transition Risks*
- Policy & legal: Pakistan carbon-pricing instrument (under discussion 2026-27), provincial NEQS tightening, EU CBAM impact on Pakistan exports
- Technology: EV penetration affecting PMG demand (Pakistan: ~1% EV today, 30% target by 2030 if subsidies hold), efficiency improvements
- Market: Customer preference shift (premium fuels, lubricants), capital reallocation by ESG-screened investors
- Reputation: ESG-rating downgrades affecting credit, NGO campaigns, community license-to-operate

### Pillar 4: Metrics & Targets
**Mandatory IFRS S2 Metrics** (S29-32):

*Scope 1, 2, 3 GHG emissions* (GHG Protocol)
- Scope 1: Direct emissions (combustion, process, fugitive) — typically 70-85% for Pakistan refineries
- Scope 2: Purchased electricity, steam, heat (typically 5-15%)
- Scope 3: 15 categories per GHG Protocol — most material for refineries are Cat 11 (Use of Sold Products = combustion of fuels by end users), Cat 1 (Purchased Goods = crude oil upstream emissions)

*Pakistan Refinery Typical Scope 1 Emissions* (per refinery, MT CO₂e/yr):
| Refinery | Throughput KBPD | Scope 1 estimate |
|---|---|---|
| Cnergyico (Hub) | 80-130 | 0.8-1.4 M MTCO₂e |
| PARCO (Mid-Country) | 100 | 0.9-1.1 M MTCO₂e |
| NRL (Karachi) | 65 | 0.6-0.7 M MTCO₂e |
| ARL (Rawalpindi) | 50-55 | 0.5-0.6 M MTCO₂e |
| PRL (Karachi) | 47-50 | 0.5-0.6 M MTCO₂e |

*Cross-industry Metrics* (IFRS S2 S29):
- GHG emissions (Scope 1+2+3)
- Climate-related risks: % of assets vulnerable; financial impact
- Climate-related opportunities: % of revenue from "low-carbon" / "green" activities (TCFD methodology)
- Capital deployment: % of capex aligned with climate
- Internal carbon price (if used; typical Pakistan O&G: USD 25-50/tCO₂ shadow pricing)
- Remuneration linked to climate (% of executive compensation)

*Industry-Specific Metrics* (IFRS S2 S32 + Industry-based Guidance for Oil & Gas):
- Methane intensity (tons CH₄ / mscf gas produced; OGMP 2.0 reporting)
- Flare intensity (mscf flared / bbl produced)
- Refinery energy intensity (Solomon EII or GJ/bbl)
- Water consumption intensity (m³ / bbl)
- Refining margin sensitivity to carbon price

**Target Setting**:
- Near-term (2025-2030): Aligned with Pakistan NDC, OGMP 2.0 methane reduction
- Long-term (2030-2050): Aligned with sector decarbonisation pathway (SBTi or equivalent)
- Recommended targets:
  - Methane intensity: ≤0.2% of gas produced (OGMP 2.0 Gold standard)
  - Flare intensity: Zero routine flaring by 2030 (World Bank ZRF initiative)
  - Scope 1+2 absolute reduction: 25-30% by 2030 vs 2020 baseline
  - 100% Euro V product slate by 2027-28 (BRP-21 alignment)

## Reporting Cadence & Assurance
- Annual disclosure in Annual Report (Pakistan Companies Act 2017 mandates separate sustainability statement for listed companies)
- Limited assurance (ISAE 3000) by Statutory Auditor or specialist (Big-4 firms have separate climate assurance practices)
- Reasonable assurance (ISAE 3410 for GHG) for Scope 1+2 mandatory for KSE-30 by FY28

## Pakistan-Specific Disclosure Considerations
- **Sovereign risk**: Pakistan's transition is highly dependent on IMF program, FX availability, foreign technical assistance — must be disclosed
- **Coal phase-out commitment**: Pakistan committed to no new coal post-2030; existing refineries should disclose stranding risk of coal-dependent units (e.g., utility boilers)
- **Hydrogen economy potential**: Pakistan's gas-rich position creates blue/green hydrogen opportunity — material for refineries with steam reformers (Cnergyico SMR, NRL)
- **Carbon market readiness**: Pakistan likely to launch voluntary carbon market 2027-28; refineries should disclose credit-eligibility positioning
