# LOPC Tier-1/Tier-2 Incident Response Playbook

## API RP 754 Tier Definitions
- **Tier 1** — Loss of Primary Containment with serious consequence (fatality, hospitalization, fire/explosion ≥USD 100k, release >threshold quantity in Table 1).
- **Tier 2** — LPC with lesser consequence (LWC injury, release > threshold quantity in Table 2, fire/explosion <USD 100k).
- **Tier 3** — Challenges to safety systems (safe operating limit excursions, demands on safety system).
- **Tier 4** — Operating discipline & management system performance (procedure compliance, training completion).

## Threshold Release Quantities (Tier 1 — kg, in any 1-hr window)
- Flammable liquid (TOC<35°C): 500 kg
- Flammable liquid (35-60°C): 1,000 kg
- Combustible liquid (60-93°C): 2,000 kg
- Flammable gas (Cat 1): 500 kg
- Toxic Category 1 (HF, Cl₂, NH₃ high-conc): 5 kg
- Toxic Category 2: 25 kg
- Toxic Category 3: 100 kg

## First 24 Hours
1. **0-15 min** — Confirm incident, declare ERP level (Level 1/2/3), evacuate, headcount, isolate sources, energize emergency lighting & PA.
2. **15-60 min** — Mobilize on-site IC, fire team, mutual-aid (Pakistan: PARCO/Cnergyico Hub corridor MoUs), notify Provincial EPA + Civil Defence + DC Office.
3. **1-4 hrs** — Stabilize hydrocarbon source, contain pool with foam/boom, conduct PPM monitoring perimeter (H₂S, LEL, VOC).
4. **4-24 hrs** — Damage assessment, photo/video evidence chain-of-custody, witness statements, preserve DCS data + alarm logs (≥30 days), preliminary regulatory filing.

## Regulatory Filings (Pakistan)
- **OGRA** — Form A within 24 hrs (refinery sector); detailed report within 30 days
- **Provincial EPA (Sindh-EPA / Punjab-EPA)** — environmental incident notification within 48 hrs, full RCA within 60 days
- **Department of Mineral & Industry (DGPC)** — for upstream LOPC, within 24 hrs
- **OGDCL/DGPC for petroleum** — incident report to Director General Petroleum Concessions
- **Civil Defence + Provincial DC** — informal notification within 4 hrs for offsite consequence
- **Pakistan Standards & Quality Control Authority (PSQCA)** — only if quality complaint linked

## RCA Methodology
- **Phase 1 (Day 1-3)** — Timeline reconstruction · 5-Whys initial hypothesis · sample collection for metallurgical/PMI analysis
- **Phase 2 (Day 4-14)** — TapRooT / Fault Tree Analysis · interviews · DCS trend extraction · mechanical integrity records pull
- **Phase 3 (Day 15-30)** — Causal chain documented · technical root cause + organizational root cause · recommendations with owner & date · lessons learned bulletin issued
- **Phase 4 (Day 31-60)** — Final report to Plant Manager + Board HSE Committee · OGRA regulatory submission · close-out tracker established

## Common LOPC Root Causes in Pakistan Refineries
| Mode | Frequency | Typical Cause |
|---|---|---|
| Crude tank floor corrosion | 35% | MIC, water phase under product, no internal coating |
| Piping deadleg corrosion | 25% | Stagnant water, sour service, CUI |
| Pump seal failure | 15% | Vibration, wear, lack of seal-flush flow |
| Heat exchanger tube rupture | 10% | Velocity erosion, vibration, sour-water service |
| Atmospheric vent flame-out | 8% | Pilot failure, condensate slug |
| FRU column overpressure | 7% | PRV undersized for fire case, control valve stuck |

## Compensation & Liability Reserves (Pakistan)
- Single fatality (worker): PKR 5-8 M minimum (court-driven), plus insurance settlement
- Property damage: deductible PKR 50-200 M typical
- Third-party off-site: separate insurance line (CGL); claim risk PKR 100M-2bn depending on community/environment impact
- Legal reserve: 1-2% of replacement cost for first 12 months post-incident
