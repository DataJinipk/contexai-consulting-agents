## Headline

ABC Refinery's three-year average refinery utilisation has hovered around 50% — less than half of global Top-Quartile peers operating above 92%. The economic cost of this utilisation gap is meaningful eight-figure USD/year in foregone gross margin, before even considering its corrosive effect on fixed-cost absorption, energy intensity, and brand credibility with crude suppliers. This Roadmap diagnoses the seven structural drivers of the gap and lays out a 60-month Operational Excellence ("OE") programme designed to lift utilisation toward 85% by year five and compress the Solomon Energy Intensity Index from an estimated bottom-quartile range into the second quartile.

The Roadmap is the operating-system prerequisite for the brownfield upgrade discussed in the parallel Strategic Option Memo. The upgrade NPV assumes 85% utilisation on Day-1 of mechanical completion; without the OE programme described here, that NPV collapses materially in the sensitivity tornado. In plainer language: the OE programme is not optional supporting infrastructure — it is the path that protects the entire upgrade investment thesis.

## The Seven Structural Drivers

ABC Refinery's utilisation gap traces to seven structural drivers, which the Roadmap addresses in priority sequence:

1. **Furnace and CDU pre-heat train fouling** (HIGH) — heat-transfer coefficient running ~22% below design; coil skin temperature trending toward maximum allowable wall temperature.

2. **Pump seal MTBF** (HIGH) — field MTBF of ~19 months versus industry Q2 of 48 months; ~41% of unplanned trips trace back to pump-seal failures.

3. **Cooling-water tower capacity** (MEDIUM) — approach temperature 8°C versus design 5°C; summer GRM compression observable in monthly operating data.

4. **Catalyst cycle length** (MEDIUM) — CCR and Hydrotreater cycle length ~70% of OEM guarantee; coke laydown imbalance suggests feed manganese poisoning.

5. **DCS alarm management** (MEDIUM) — current alarm load ~280/operator/hour versus EEMUA-191 target of <12/hour during stable operation; alarm flood masking real incidents.

6. **Crude diet variability** (MEDIUM) — slate switches >9 distinct grades/year versus peer Q2 of 4-5; transition losses ~0.4 vol% per switch.

7. **Operator competency / shift culture** (HIGH) — senior board-operator time-on-job trending down (retirements); structured knowledge-transfer gap.

Drivers #1, #2 and #7 — collectively ~55% of the utilisation gap — are addressable without major capex. They yield the fastest payback and are the focus of Year-1 of the Roadmap.

## The Pump MTBF Programme (Year 1 Priority)

The single most economically attractive reliability initiative is the pump MTBF lift. Current field MTBF of 19 months reflects under-investment in seal selection, alignment discipline, and operator-led condition monitoring. Industry Q2 sits at 48 months and Q1 at 60+ months. The lift is driven by:

| Lever | Current | Target | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Seal selection — API 682 Plan 53B uptake | Mixed; ~40% non-682 | 100% API-682 compliant | Replace at next overhaul cycle |
| Pump alignment — laser tools | Dial-indicator basis | Laser; <0.05 mm | Tool acquisition + 6-week training cycle |
| Vibration monitoring | Quarterly walks | Bi-weekly + online for critical | Wireless sensors (ABB / Emerson) |
| Lubrication — Particle Count + Water | Calendar-based | Condition-based | ISO 4406 sampling discipline |
| Operator rounds (Watchkeeper) | Paper-form | Digital + escalation | Mobile app on ATEX-rated tablets |

We model a non-linear MTBF response — 19 months → 28 months by end-Year 1, 38 months by end-Year 2, and the 48-month target inside 36 months. The economic uplift from this curve alone is several USD M/year at steady state from reduced spare-part consumption, reduced operator-hours on corrective work, and fewer cascade outages.

## RBI Adoption (API RP 580 / 581)

ABC Refinery's current inspection cadence is broadly time-based — typically 4-yearly internal inspection regardless of damage-mechanism likelihood. Migrating to Risk-Based Inspection will redirect inspection effort to the 20% of pressure-equipment items that drive ~80% of risk, allowing extension of intervals on the remaining 80% (and freeing TAR scope budget for higher-value scopes such as furnace re-tubing).

The Roadmap sequences this as: P-0 Mobilise (M0-M2) → P-1 Data collation (M2-M6) → P-2 Likelihood/COF model (M6-M10) → P-3 Plan rebuild (M10-M14) → P-4 Sustain (M14+). RBI vendor selection (DNV / Lloyd's Register / TUV / equivalent) is a Year-1 priority.

## Operations Excellence Council (Governance)

The OE programme is governed by a newly constituted Operations Excellence Council ("OEC"), chaired jointly by the CEO and COO. Members include the VP Refining, VP HSE, GM Reliability, GM Operations, GM Engineering, and the CIO. The OEC meets monthly (90-minute stand-ups) and conducts quarterly half-day deep-dives. The KPI dashboard is reported to the Board Audit Committee and the BSCC every quarter.

**Critical structural move**: the OEC KPI suite (EII, MTBF, utilisation, LOPC, TRIR) is linked to CEO and COO long-term-incentive vesting from Year 1 — without that linkage, the Council's recommendations risk being deprioritised in favour of capex-driven projects with louder Board visibility.
