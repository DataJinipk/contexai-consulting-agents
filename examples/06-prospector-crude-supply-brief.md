## The Diagnosis

ABC Refinery today runs 100% imported crude, with ~91% from just two suppliers (the regional national oil company term, plus one additional GCC counterparty). Zero indigenous Pakistan crude features in the diet, despite Pakistan producing 70-75 KBD of light-sweet domestic streams that would yield positively on ABC Refinery's CDU/VDU configuration. Strategic crude inventory sits at 14 days — comfortable for routine operations but thin against the geopolitical realities of the Strait of Hormuz, Bab al-Mandab (Houthi attacks have cost regional shipping 2-3 weeks of additional transit), and seasonal monsoon SPM disruption. SPM-2 (VLCC-receivable) is mechanically complete but uncommissioned.

That single-source concentration is the largest unaddressed supply-chain risk in the company.

## Direction 2 — The Indigenous Crude Prize (Detail)

Pakistan produces ~70-75 KBD of crude domestically, primarily from three operators:

| Field / source | Operator | API (°) | S (wt%) | Available volume (KBD) | Logistics to refinery |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tal Block (Manzalai, Makori, Mami Khel) | OGDCL | 38-43 | 0.05-0.20 | 30-35 | Rail from Kohat → Karachi |
| Rajian field | OGDCL | 44-46 | 0.05 | 5-7 | Truck → Karachi rail head |
| Adhi field | PPL | 38-40 | 0.10 | 8-10 | Truck → Karachi |
| Kandhkot | PPL | 36-38 | 0.10 | 3-4 | Truck |
| HRL / Bobi (Mari area) | MPL | 34-36 | 0.20 | 4-5 | Truck |

Realistic ABC Refinery offtake target: 8-12 KBD (5-8% of throughput post-upgrade), focused on Tal Block rail-deliverable cargoes. The economic case rests on three pillars: (a) light naphtha credit — Pakistan crudes yield 22-28% straight-run naphtha versus 18-22% for Arab Light; (b) low sulphur means lower SRU loading and Euro-V spec compliance ease; (c) PKR-denominated procurement — a major FX hedge given ABC Refinery's USD-denominated crude bill versus PKR-denominated product sales.

Three contract structures are available:

| Structure | Mechanism | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Direct term offtake from OGDCL (5-yr term, monthly cargoes) | Stable supply; PKR settlement; relationship | Pricing typically tied to Arab Light formula |
| B | Swap with PRL/ARL (ABC gets indigenous; they get imported) | Logistics simpler; mutual benefit | Counterparty constraint; volume-limited |
| C | Equity investment in Tal Block (5-10% working interest) | Long-term margin capture; upstream exposure | Capex commitment USD 80-140 M |

Recommended structure: **A + C** — direct term offtake supplemented by 5% working interest in Tal Block. The combined capex is USD 90-110 M, with payback ~6-8 years from working-interest cash flows alone.

## Direction 3 — The Russian Urals Special Case

Russian Urals continues to trade at USD 10-15/bbl discount to Brent in 2026 as a function of Western sanctions architecture and limited buyer pool. This is the largest crude arbitrage in modern history and is materially under-played by Pakistani refiners to date. The constraints are real but navigable:

- **Sanctions architecture:** G7 price-cap mechanism allows non-cap-compliant lifting via non-G7 insurance + flag — many Asian buyers (India, China, Turkey, UAE) participate routinely;
- **Banking:** PKR or AED settlement via non-Western correspondent banks; some lifts settle in CNY;
- **Reputational / policy risk:** requires SBP comfort + Foreign Office heads-up; precedent exists at PRL for similar volumes;
- **Vessel availability:** "shadow fleet" tankers must be IACS-class compliant; quality of crude has been generally consistent (Primorsk loading).

Our recommendation: target 4-6 Urals cargoes in FY26 (~3-5 MMbbl total, or ~6-8% of annual barrels) via established non-G7 trading houses. Conservative arbitrage capture USD 2.5/bbl × 5 MMbbl = USD 12.5 M one-off. The arbitrage window will close — likely 2027 as sanctions architecture evolves — but the FY26-FY27 window is genuinely free money for refiners who can take it.

## Direction 4 — Strategic Inventory (14 → 32 days)

Strategic inventory has carrying cost (financing + storage) but generates option value during disruption windows. The historical 10-year record shows three episodes where days-of-stock fell to ≤6 days and emergency spot purchases were required at USD 3-8/bbl premium over term contract. A 32-day buffer eliminates this risk class.

Net economic case: incremental financing cost (low USD M/year) is materially less than the avoided-emergency-purchase premium and uptime protection (mid-eight-figure USD/year). The Roadmap commits to the inventory uplift in two stages — to 22 days within existing tankage by end-FY26, and to 32 days by end-FY28 (requires modest tankage augmentation, USD 35-45 M capex).

Strategic inventory is by definition price-exposed. Recommendation: institute a rolling Brent-paper hedge on the upper 50% of strategic stock. This converts the strategic-stock decision from a price-bet into a pure availability-insurance instrument.
